A rice dispenser vending machine is a convenient and hygienic way to dispense rice in a public or commercial setting. This type of machine is typically found in Asian countries where rice is a staple food. The rice dispenser vending machine consists of a cabinet that holds a large quantity of rice. The rice is stored in a container with a dispenser mechanism that allows customers to select the amount of rice they need. The machine is usually operated by inserting coins or bills into a slot or using a contactless payment method such as a credit card or mobile payment app. To use the machine, the customer selects the desired amount of rice by pressing the appropriate button on a keypad or touchscreen. The machine dispenses the rice into a container or bag, which the customer can take away with them. The benefits of a rice dispenser vending machine include the convenience of being able to purchase rice quickly and easily, especially in busy locations where there may be limited options for purchasing rice. The machine is also hygienic, as it reduces the need for customers to handle the rice directly, minimizing the risk of contamination. In addition, the rice dispenser vending machine can be programmed to dispense different types of rice, such as brown rice or sushi rice, and can be set to dispense different portion sizes to accommodate the needs of different customers. Overall, the rice dispenser vending machine is a useful and innovative solution for providing convenient access to rice in a commercial or public setting.

MANILA, PHILIPPINES — Rice production in the Philippines in 2022-23 will stay flat with last year’s record at 12.4 million tonnes, according to a report from the Foreign Agricultural Service of the US Department of Agriculture (USDA). Rice imports of 2.8 million tonnes will be 200,000 tonnes lower than 2021-22. Wheat imports are forecast to decline 3% to 6.3 million tonnes because of rising prices and supply issues as a result of the conflict in the Black Sea. Consumption for 2022-23 is down 350,000 tonnes, to 6.25 million tonnes, due to high wheat prices. Feed demand is estimated at 2.4 million tonnes, down 300,000 tonnes. “Factors include the ongoing Black Sea conflict and efforts by local feed millers to replace wheat with barley because of rising feed wheat prices,” the USDA said. “Uncertainty also remains in hog feed demand due to ongoing African swine fever spread.” Corn is expected to supplement some feed wheat demand, while milling wheat consumption is seen slightly declining for more affordable rice. The USDA expects 2022-23 corn imports will rise 50,000 tonnes to 750,000 tonnes, as feed wheat prices are elevated by the Ukraine conflict. Corn production in 2022-23 is forecast at 8.3 million tonnes, rebounding after the previous year’s subpar growing conditions in three out of the five top corn regions. - Susan Reidy
